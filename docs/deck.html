The problem · 02

India is one chokepoint away from a price shock.

Import dependence88%of crude consumption is imported.
Via Strait of Hormuz40–45%of those imports transit one strait.
Strategic reserve cover9.5 daysof strategic SPR — a thin buffer.
A single Gulf disruption cascades within days to refinery run-cuts, landed-cost spikes, and pump-price pass-through — and today the response is manual, siloed, and slow (a ~47-day stabilization gap).
SENTINELsource: ET Hackathon brief PS2 · PPAC
Why it's hard today · 03

The signals exist. The decision loop doesn't.

Signals

News, AIS vessel tracks, prices and sanctions live in separate silos — no one score that says "Hormuz is heating up."

Scenarios

Impact planning is spreadsheet-bound and opaque — assumptions buried, hard to audit under time pressure.

Procurement

Rerouting is tribal knowledge — which grade fits which refinery, at what cost, by when?

Nobody owns the continuous path from a signal to an executable decision. Sentinel is that path.

Business impact · 10

Lead time is the buffer.

With only ~9.5 days of strategic cover, the scarce resource in a crude crisis is decision time. Compressing signal-to-reroute from days to minutes protects SPR runway and blunts the ₹/L pass-through before it reaches the pump.
Decision quality matters too: the grade-compatibility filter kills infeasible cargoes procurement would otherwise chase — every card is liftable at a named refinery.
Response time< 5 minsignal → executable recommendation, vs a ~47-day manual gap.
Every recommendationgrade-checkedAPI° / sulfur validated per shortfall refinery.
